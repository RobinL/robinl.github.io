# Visualising the Fellegi-Sunter model

The [mathematics article](/maths_of_fellegi_sunter/) presented the Fellegi-Sunter model and showed that match probability can be represented as:

$$
\operatorname{Pr}(\text{records match}\mid\gamma_1,\ldots,\gamma_K)
=\frac{\lambda m_{1\ell}\cdots m_{K\ell}}
{\lambda m_{1\ell}\cdots m_{K\ell}+(1-\lambda)u_{1\ell}\cdots u_{K\ell}}
$$

This article presents a way to understand and visualise that formula. We take each piece of evidence into account in turn, using the model's assumption that comparison columns are conditionally independent given match status.

The example uses four comparison columns: `fname` (first name), `sname` (surname), `dob` (date of birth), and `town`. The default parameter values are deliberately chosen to keep the diagrams legible.

## Annex: mathematical representation

The step-by-step calculation is a repeated application of Bayes' theorem:

```text
prior = lambda
for column in comparison_columns:
  posterior = bayes(column, prior)
  prior = posterior
```

For two columns, first apply Bayes' theorem to the first comparison:

$$
t=\operatorname{Pr}(\text{match}\mid\gamma_1)
=\frac{\lambda m_{1\ell}}{\lambda m_{1\ell}+(1-\lambda)u_{1\ell}}
$$

This posterior becomes the prior for the second comparison:

$$
\operatorname{Pr}(\text{match}\mid\gamma_1,\gamma_2)
=\frac{t m_{2\ell}}{t m_{2\ell}+(1-t)u_{2\ell}}
$$

Substituting the expression for $t$ and cancelling the common denominator gives:

$$
\operatorname{Pr}(\text{match}\mid\gamma_1,\gamma_2)
=\frac{\lambda m_{1\ell}m_{2\ell}}
{\lambda m_{1\ell}m_{2\ell}+(1-\lambda)u_{1\ell}u_{2\ell}}
$$
