> One of the things Daisy Christodoulis said to me a while ago, and I think she's written about this, is that explanations are massively overrated.
>
> You can spend five, 10 minutes doing an explanation, and there are a couple of issues with that. One, the kids are likely to be overwhelmed by it. Two, you're getting no data back whatsoever. And three, you've just got a real inefficiency problem because you can cut all those words out.
>
> As long as you present carefully sequenced examples and non-examples with minimal changes, or whatever it may be, and you're careful with your selection, you can do a whole lot better in a lot less time.

From [Mr Barton Maths Podcast #226: AI in Education with Zach Groshell](https://podcasters.spotify.com/pod/show/mrbartonmaths/episodes/226-AI-in-Education-with-Zach-Groshell-e3lgmj5).
