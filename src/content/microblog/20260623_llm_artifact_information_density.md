---
title: "LLM artefacts and information density"
date: "2026-06-23"
tags: ["AI", "Data", "Visualisation"]
---

The artefact you ask for from your LLM should be the highest information density representation of the answer you want. That's often an interactive webapp with diagrams and visualisations.

However, one of the biggest shortcomings of LLMs is they are pretty terrible at pulling insights from data. That is the true skill of experts like John Burn-Murdoch at the FT e.g. see [here](https://x.com/jburnmurdoch/status/1641799627128143873), not beautiful visualisation.

So if the insights are still largely in the hands of the human using the LLM, it's up to them to learn how to tease them out of the model.
