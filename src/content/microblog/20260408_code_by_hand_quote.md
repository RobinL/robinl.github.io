---
title: "Code by hand and the idea bottleneck"
date: "2026-04-08"
tags: ["AI", "Coding", "Product"]
---

> Pretty clear that writing code by hand is mostly a thing of the past. But it's still hard to have good ideas, and to decide how a thing should work, and what you actually want. You can deliver a functional app in a single prompt, but it takes another 50 or 100 and many hours to finesse. It still surprises me there hasn't been a massive explosion in free apps. Or perhaps they're just hard to find.
>
> I've barely written any code by hand since about November, and there's no sign of progress slowing down. Hard to believe models won't be superhuman at coding in almost all respects within a couple of years, if not sooner.
